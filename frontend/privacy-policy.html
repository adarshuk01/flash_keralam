1. Overview

The NEWS* ("we", "us", or "our") operates the website thenews.com (the "Site"). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our Site, subscribe to our newsletters, or interact with us in any way.

By accessing or using our Site, you agree to the terms of this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of the Site.

We are committed to protecting your personal information in compliance with applicable data protection laws, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), and other applicable laws.

2. Data We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

  • Contact form submissions — name, email address, message content, and subject
  • Newsletter sign-ups — email address and name
  • Comments or feedback — any content you submit through our interactive features
  • Account registration (if applicable) — username, email, password (hashed)

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you visit our Site, certain information is collected automatically through cookies and similar tracking technologies:

  • Log data — IP address, browser type and version, operating system, referring URL, pages visited, time and date of visit, time spent on pages
  • Device information — device type, screen resolution, language settings
  • Usage data — articles read, videos watched, links clicked, scroll depth
  • Cookies and similar tracking — see Section 4 for full details

2.3 Information from Third Parties

We may receive information about you from third-party sources, including advertising partners, analytics providers (such as Google Analytics), and social media platforms if you interact with our content on those platforms.

3. How We Use Your Data

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To operate, maintain, and improve our Site and services
  • To respond to your enquiries, comments, or requests submitted via our contact form
  • To send you editorial newsletters and updates (with your consent)
  • To personalise your experience on our Site
  • To understand how our audience interacts with our content (analytics)
  • To display relevant advertisements, including through Google AdSense
  • To detect, prevent, and address technical issues, fraud, or security breaches
  • To comply with our legal obligations
  • To enforce our Terms of Service

Legal basis (GDPR): We rely on legitimate interests for analytics and site operation; consent for marketing emails and personalised advertising; and legal obligation for compliance-related processing.

4. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

We use cookies (small text files stored on your device) and similar technologies such as web beacons and pixel tags to operate and improve our Site and to deliver relevant advertising.

4.1 Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential cookies — Required for the Site to function (e.g., session management, dark/light mode preference). Cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics cookies — Help us understand how visitors use the Site (e.g., Google Analytics). Collect anonymised data about traffic and behaviour.
  • Advertising cookies — Used by Google AdSense and our other advertising partners to serve personalised ads based on your browsing history across websites.
  • Functional cookies — Remember your preferences such as language or newsletter subscription status.

4.2 Managing Cookies

You can control or disable cookies at any time through your browser settings. Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of the Site. For more information on managing cookies, visit allaboutcookies.org.

To opt out of Google Analytics tracking, you can install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

5. Google AdSense & Advertising

Important: This section specifically addresses our use of Google AdSense and is required under Google's publisher policies. Please read it carefully.

We use Google AdSense, a service provided by Google LLC ("Google"), to display advertisements on our Site. Google AdSense uses cookies and similar technologies to show you ads that are relevant to your interests.

5.1 How Google AdSense Works

  • Google uses the DoubleClick cookie and other cookies to serve ads based on your prior visits to our Site and other websites on the internet.
  • Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads based on your visit to our Site and/or other sites on the internet.
  • Ads served by Google may appear as display ads, text ads, or other formats across our pages.

5.2 Data Google Collects for Advertising

When Google AdSense is active on our pages, Google may collect:

  • Your IP address (may be used to infer approximate location)
  • Browser and device information
  • Cookie identifiers associated with your browser or device
  • Pages and content you view on our Site
  • Information about your interests inferred from your browsing behaviour across the web

5.3 Opting Out of Personalised Advertising

You can opt out of personalised advertising from Google at any time by visiting:

  • Google Ads Settings
  • Digital Advertising Alliance (DAA) Opt-Out
  • Your Online Choices (EU)

Please note that opting out of personalised advertising does not mean you will stop seeing ads. You will still see ads, but they will not be personalised based on your interests.

5.4 Google's Privacy Policy

Google's use of data collected via AdSense is governed by Google's Privacy Policy. We encourage you to review it to understand how Google processes your data.

Note for EEA/UK users: Where required by law, we obtain your consent before placing non-essential cookies (including advertising cookies) on your device. You may withdraw consent at any time through our cookie preference centre or your browser settings.

Our Site integrates with the following third-party services that may collect information about you independently:

  • Google Analytics — Website traffic and user behaviour analytics. Privacy Policy
  • Google AdSense — Advertising platform. Privacy Policy
  • YouTube — Embedded video content. Privacy Policy
  • Social media sharing buttons — Twitter/X, Facebook, LinkedIn, Instagram. These platforms may set cookies when you interact with sharing buttons.

We do not control how these third parties collect or use your data. We recommend reviewing each provider's privacy policy directly.

7. Data Sharing & Disclosure

We do not sell your personal information to third parties. We may share your data in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service providers — Trusted vendors who help us operate the Site (hosting, email delivery, analytics) under strict data processing agreements.
  • Advertising partners — As described in Section 5, Google and other ad partners receive data necessary to serve advertisements.
  • Legal requirements — When required by law, court order, or to protect our legal rights.
  • Business transfers — In connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your data may be transferred as a business asset.
  • Safety — To protect the safety of any person or to prevent fraud or illegal activity.

8. Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected:

  • Contact form data — Retained for up to 24 months, then securely deleted.
  • Newsletter subscriptions — Retained until you unsubscribe, plus 30 days.
  • Analytics data — Anonymised data retained for up to 26 months (Google Analytics default).
  • Server logs — Retained for up to 90 days for security and debugging purposes.
  • Cookie data — Duration varies per cookie type; see your browser settings for details.

9. Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right to access — Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification — Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten") — Request deletion of your personal data.
  • Right to restrict processing — Ask us to limit how we use your data.
  • Right to data portability — Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to object — Object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing.
  • Right to withdraw consent — Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at privacy@thenews.com. We will respond within 30 days (or the applicable legal timeframe).

10. GDPR — European Economic Area & UK

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA) or the United Kingdom, the following applies:

  • The data controller responsible for your data is The NEWS*, 1 Press Plaza, Suite 500, New York, NY 10001, USA.
  • We transfer data outside the EEA/UK only where appropriate safeguards exist (e.g., EU Standard Contractual Clauses, adequacy decisions).
  • You have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority (e.g., the ICO in the UK or your national supervisory authority in the EU).
  • We obtain explicit consent before placing non-essential cookies on your device where required.

11. CCPA — California Residents

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) grants you the following additional rights:

  • Right to Know — Request disclosure of the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you in the past 12 months.
  • Right to Delete — Request deletion of your personal information.
  • Right to Opt-Out of Sale — We do not sell personal information. However, sharing data with advertising partners for cross-context behavioural advertising may constitute a "sale" under CCPA. You may opt out via aboutads.info.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination — We will not discriminate against you for exercising any CCPA rights.

To submit a CCPA request, email us at privacy@thenews.com with the subject line "CCPA Request".

12. Children's Privacy

Our Site is not directed to children under the age of 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal data, please contact us at privacy@thenews.com and we will delete the information promptly.

In compliance with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), we do not knowingly display personalised advertising to children under 13.

13. Data Security

We implement industry-standard technical and organisational measures to protect your personal information from unauthorised access, loss, misuse, or alteration. These include:

  • HTTPS/TLS encryption for all data in transit
  • Encrypted storage for sensitive data at rest
  • Access controls limiting data access to authorised personnel only
  • Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments

However, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your data, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

14. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal requirements, or other factors. When we make material changes, we will:

  • Update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page
  • Post a prominent notice on our Site
  • Where required by law, notify you by email or obtain renewed consent

We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically to stay informed about how we are protecting your information.
